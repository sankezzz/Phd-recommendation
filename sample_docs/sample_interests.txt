============================================================
CV 1 — Arjun Mehta (NLP / LLM)
============================================================

I want to work on making large language models more reliable and
grounded — specifically reducing hallucination in long-form generation
and improving factual consistency in retrieval-augmented systems. I am
particularly interested in low-resource and multilingual settings, where
most current LLM work does not generalise well. My background in
code-switched speech and cross-lingual transfer gives me a different
angle on generalisation that I think applies directly to multilingual LLM
alignment. I want to work with a lab that is pushing the boundaries of
how we evaluate and control what LLMs actually know versus what they
confabulate.


============================================================
CV 2 — Priya Nair (Computational Biology / Genomics)
============================================================

I want to apply deep learning to understand gene regulation — specifically
how DNA sequence encodes cell-type-specific chromatin accessibility and
transcription factor binding. My thesis work on predicting ATAC-seq peaks
from sequence showed me how much signal is still left unexploited in the
raw genome. I am interested in scaling these models to whole-genome
resolution and applying them to rare disease variant interpretation. I want
to join a lab that works at the intersection of machine learning and
functional genomics, ideally with access to large patient cohorts or
biobank data.


============================================================
CV 3 — Lucas Ferreira (Computer Vision / Robotics)
============================================================

I want to work on 3D scene understanding for autonomous systems — 
specifically how robots can build persistent, semantically rich maps of
environments from partial and noisy sensor data. My experience with LiDAR
point cloud processing and monocular depth estimation gives me a strong
foundation in both sensor modalities. I am particularly interested in
combining neural radiance fields or Gaussian splatting with SLAM for
real-time dense mapping on resource-constrained platforms. I want to work
with a lab that deploys research on real robotic hardware, not just
simulation benchmarks.
